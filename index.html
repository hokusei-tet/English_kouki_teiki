"I made it!" On October 22, 2019, a young baker, Shuichi Osawa, shouted with joy in Nantes, France.
He had just won first prize at the World of Bread Contest.
On the podium where all the members of his team were standing, he gave a big smile and said,"I am truly proud. All my effort has finally paid off."
The contest is held every two years.
In 2019, teams from several countries gathered.
Each team consists of a baker, an assistant, and a coach.
To meet the standards of the contest, all the bread must be delicious and also pleasing to the eye.
The contest was a big challenge with lots of requirements, but Osawa ded it.
This was the very first time a Japanese team had won the contest.
Zoos are considered not only entertainment facilities but also education centers.
動物園は娯楽施設だけでなく、教育センターとしても位置づけられています。
They offer visitors opportunities to learn about animals through programs and activities.
彼らはプログラムやアクティビティを通じて、来園者に動物について学ぶ機会を提供しています。
You can meet small animals in petting areas.
ふれあいコーナーでは小動物たちと触れ合うことができます。
You are given the chance to touch, smell, or watch them in those areas.
これらのエリアでは、触ったり、匂いを嗅いだり、観察したりする機会が与えられます。
Zookeepers give you a lecture on each animal and its habitat as well.
飼育員があなたにそれぞれの動物とその生息地について解説してくれます。
All of these zoo activities are designed for visitors to understand and expand their knowledge about many animals in our world.
これらの動物園のアクティビティはすべて、来場者が世界中の多くの動物についての理解を深め、知識を広げられるように設計されています。
Zoos educate millions of visitors every year.
動物園は毎年何百万人もの来場者を教育しています。
Education is one of the main priorities for zoos.
教育は動物園にとって主要な優先事項の一つです。
Zoos also have a strong emphasis on scientific research and species conservation.
