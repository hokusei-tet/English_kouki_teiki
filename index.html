英語 後期定期範囲 まとめ

"I made it!" On October 22, 2019, a young baker, Shuichi Osawa, shouted with joy in Nantes, France.
｢やった！｣2019年10月22日、フランスのナントで若いパン職人、大澤秀一が喜びの叫びを上げました。
He had just won first prize at the World of Bread Contest.
彼は世界パンコンテストで優勝したばかりでした。
On the podium where all the members of his team were standing, he gave a big smile and said,"I am truly proud. All my effort has finally paid off."
チーム全員が立つ表彰台で、彼は満面の笑みを浮かべ、「本当に誇りに思います。これまで(すべて)の努力がついに報われました。」と言いました。
The contest is held every two years.
この大会は2年に一度開催されます。
In 2019, teams from several countries gathered.
2019年には、複数の国からチームが集まりました。
Each team consists of a baker, an assistant, and a coach.
各チームは、パン職人、アシスタント、コーチで構成されています。
To meet the standards of the contest, all the bread must be delicious and also pleasing to the eye.
コンテストの基準を満たすには、すべてのパンが美味しく、見た目も美しくなければなりませんでした。
The contest was a big challenge with lots of requirements, but Osawa ded it.
このコンテストは多くの要件を伴う大きな挑戦でしたが、大澤はそれを成し遂げました。
This was the very first time a Japanese team had won the contest.
日本のチームがコンテンストで優勝したのはこれが始めてでした。
Osawa said, "My family runs a bakery, so it was natural for me to become a baker."
In 2012, after working as a baker for several years, he left his hometown in Gunma, heading for Kobe to ask the famous chef Takaaki Nisikawa for training.
"I was really amazed to see how he kneaded dough. He treated it just like a new born baby," said Osawa.
"He was very strict with me, but I swore to myself I would never give up."
In 2015, Nishikawa took Osawa to Nantes.
Osawa had a chance to see the World of Bread Contest, overwhelmed by the international bakers' skills and passion.
He said to himself, "I'll enter this contest some day."
Returning from France, Osawa was full of passion and started preparing for the contest.
According to the regulations, each team has to make eleven types of bread within eight and a half hours.
He had to practice hard and needed a place to practice.
In 2017, a coffee shop owner kindly offered him a storage place where he could practice.
"I was very lucky because people around me always helped me, so I managed to continue my challenge," said Osawa.
He was delighted at the offer, but he had several problems, including an electrical power shortage and a kitchen too small for an oven.
In the storage place, the temperature reached as high as 50℃ in summer,and it was freezing in winter.
He worked during the daytime and practiced ata night.
He could hardly sleep.
His life was like that for almost two years.
At last, Osawa arrived back in Nantes, where the 2019 World of Bread Contest would be held.
He was the ambassador of bread from Japan.
As soon as the contest started, he began to make the required types of bread one after another.
All the beautiful bread Osawa had made grabbed the judges' hearts.
Above all, his original work "Yabusame" simply amazed the whole venue.
Osawa's team won the championship.
Some judges said, "You're incredible and just crazy!"
Osawa was overjoyed to hear the praise.
Osawa said, "I won, but it doesn't mean I have special talents. I did well only because I practiced a lot."
Practice males perfect.
He did win the international contest, but his story of baking the best bread will never end.
